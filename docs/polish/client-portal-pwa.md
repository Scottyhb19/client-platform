## 0. Executive summary

The portal is **functionally complete and shipped-quality on its periphery** — the week strip, per-day card state machine (seven states, server-derived), completion capture, reports, booking, messages, and the `you` account surface all work, with correct RLS scoping, genuinely defensive realtime (visibility-change resync), idempotent session start/resume, and correct completion-stats math. The prior pass did real work and most of it should not be touched.

The gaps cluster in two places, and one of them is the surface that matters most.

**1. The in-session logging screen has quietly diverged from the brief and the validated prototype — on the named, load-bearing surface.** §6.3.1 and `client-portal.html` specify a **dark, gym-friendly** screen that **groups supersets/tri-sets on one screen**, shows an **expandable video thumbnail per exercise**, and ends with a **per-group RPE slider + notes** and a **"Great work, [Name]"** session-RPE screen. The production Logger is **light**, walks **one exercise at a time** regardless of superset grouping, shows **no video** (even though the RPC already returns the URL), captures **per-set numeric RPE** instead of a per-group slider, has **no per-group notes**, and says **"All the work is in."** It works — but it is not the screen the brief describes, and this is the screen a beta tester actually experiences mid-session in a gym. This is the heart of the section and the subject of the one big decision (Q1).

**2. A timezone-correctness fault runs through the portal's date logic.** Every "now" in the server-rendered Today/Day screen is a bare `new Date()` — UTC on Vercel. For an Australian client, from local midnight until ~10–11am AEST the portal believes today is *yesterday*. This is not cosmetic: `deriveDayState` mis-files **today's** session as `future-scheduled`, so before mid-morning the primary CTA reads **"Begin session early"** behind a confirmation modal instead of **"Begin session"**, and yesterday's card claims to be today. The greeting period, the week anchor, and the week-number are all UTC too. Section 6 left this as a rider and already built the fix.

Two production-grade security/infra items round it out: the portal owns ~7 client-facing **SECURITY DEFINER** RPCs whose anon-EXECUTE posture needs the same live-grant verification section 6 did for the calendar family (the Supabase auto-grant trap re-trips on every `CREATE OR REPLACE`); and **offline in-session logging is a deliberate no-op** (sw.js), which is the single most likely *real-world* failure for a gym PWA — accepted under the prior B3 watch-list trigger, but named here because section 7 is the launch surface.

Everything else is polish: token drift concentrated in the Logger, a permanently-dead "Avg" stat, a raw `alert()` on completion failure, and small copy deviations from the brief's voice.

### What was checked and is **correct** (regression-protect — do not "fix")

- **Auth + RLS scoping** throughout; self-only `clients` lookup; SECURITY DEFINER RPCs pinned to `auth.uid()`; no `service_role` in the portal.
- **Session start/resume idempotency** ([`actions.ts`](../../src/app/portal/session/[dayId]/actions.ts) `startOrResumeSessionAction`) and the Phase-I page-level completion guard ([`session/[dayId]/page.tsx:38-49`](../../src/app/portal/session/[dayId]/page.tsx)).
- **Per-set optimistic logging** via `client_log_set`; **completion stats math** ([`complete/page.tsx`](../../src/app/portal/session/[dayId]/complete/page.tsx)).
- **Realtime + visibility-change resync** ([`BottomNav.tsx:42-76`](../../src/app/portal/_components/BottomNav.tsx)) — the iOS WebSocket-suspension defence. Reproduce, never replace.
- **Completion *capture* is wired** — `completeSessionAction` passes feedback + session RPE through (not `null, null`); Phase C did ship. The gap is the *shape* of the end-of-session flow, not the data path.
- **Video data is already plumbed** — `client_get_program_day_exercises` v2 returns `exercise_video_url` ([`20260507100100`](../../supabase/migrations/20260507100100_client_get_program_day_exercises_v2.sql) line 74). The video gap is **UI-only**.

**Reschedule false-collision — root cause (read from the SQL, not inferred).** `client_reschedule_program_day_to_today` (migrations [`20260513140000`](../../supabase/migrations/20260513140000_client_reschedule_program_day_to_today.sql) / [`20260513150000`](../../supabase/migrations/20260513150000_client_reschedule_program_day_to_today_v2.sql)) sets `v_today date := CURRENT_DATE` (v1:83 / v2:51). On Supabase, `CURRENT_DATE` resolves in the **UTC** database session timezone — not the EP's/client's local date. So the RPC's "today" can be a different calendar day from the UI's "today," and refusal (d) — *"Today already has a session"* — tests `pd2.scheduled_date = v_today` (the UTC today) against the client's days; near the local↔UTC date boundary the move target and the collision check land on the wrong date, so the refusal fires (or the move lands on the wrong day) even though the client's *local* today is genuinely empty. Same UTC root as the rest of FM-1, one layer down. Fix: stop deriving "today" from UTC inside the RPC — resolve it in the device/org timezone in the server action and pass it in as `p_today`; the RPC keeps all its ownership + in-progress + completed guards. The exact reproduction depends on the time-of-day it was tapped; the root cause is unambiguous from the SQL.
